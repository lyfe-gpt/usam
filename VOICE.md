# USAM Fund — Voice & Tone

How USAM Fund sounds in writing. Pairs with `DESIGN.md` (how it looks). The model is
**Better.com's plain-spoken, anti-jargon challenger voice**, adapted for an audience of
real estate investors and the "Hard money. Soft terms." redesign. When you write or edit
any copy — a headline, a button, an email, a guide — it should pass the checks below.

---

## Who we're talking to

Real estate investors, operators, and developers. They are sophisticated, time-pressured,
and deal-driven. They don't need hand-holding; they need a fast, straight answer and a lender
who thinks like they do. Write investor-to-investor, never bank-to-applicant.

## The one-line standard

> Say the true thing, plainly, the way a sharp investor would say it to a peer.

If a sentence sounds like a bank disclosure, a brochure, or a hype ad, it's wrong.

---

## Voice pillars

**1. Direct & plain-spoken.** Short sentences. Active voice. Lead with the point. Cut filler
("in order to" → "to"; "we are able to" → "we can"). If a 12-year-old investor couldn't follow
it, simplify.

**2. Investor-minded.** Talk in deals and numbers — purchase, rehab, ARV, leverage, exit — not
"financial solutions." We've been in the deal; it shows.

**3. Human & accessible.** Real people answer the phone. No portals, no runaround, no call-center
energy. Warm, but never cutesy.

**4. Confident, not hype.** We know we're good, so we don't shout. State capability flatly and let
it land. Avoid exclamation points, superlatives ("best", "#1", "revolutionary"), and empty
intensifiers ("very", "extremely").

**5. Honest & transparent.** Name the trade-offs ("a higher rate than a bank, in exchange for
speed"). Keep the guardrails visible: business-purpose only, not a commitment to lend, typical
terms subject to underwriting, no hard credit pull to start.

**6. Fast & decisive.** The copy should feel like momentum — quick answers, clear next steps,
"get a real rate in minutes."

---

## Mechanics

- **Person:** second person ("you", "your deal") and first-person plural ("we fund", "we decide").
- **Contractions:** yes — "we're", "you'll", "don't". They keep it human.
- **Sentences:** mostly short. Vary rhythm; a 3-word sentence is fine for punch.
- **Numbers:** numerals for figures and ranges — "90%", "5-7 days", "$10M", "DSCR 1.00".
- **Jargon:** use the investor terms (DSCR, ARV, LTV, bridge) but define on first/heavy use.
  Never use bank/corporate jargon ("solutions", "leverage synergies", "best-in-class").
- **Punctuation:** **no en dashes** (`–`). Use hyphens or em dashes (`—`). Ranges use "to" or a
  hyphen. Minimal exclamation points (roughly zero).
- **Capitalization:** sentence case for most UI; Title Case only for proper program names.
- **Legal line:** "Loans for business purpose only · Not a commitment to lend."

## Word choices

| Say this | Not this |
|---|---|
| We fund / we decide / we close | "Solutions are provided" / passive voice |
| Real people who answer the phone | "Dedicated account representatives" |
| Get a real rate in minutes | "Request a personalized quote today!" |
| No hard credit pull to start | "Soft inquiry only at this stage of the process" |
| Deals that don't fit a box | "Non-standard credit profiles" |
| A higher rate, in exchange for speed | (hiding the trade-off) |
| Talk to us | "Contact our team" |
| Up to 90% of purchase | "Industry-leading leverage" |

**Banned words/phrases:** best-in-class, world-class, revolutionary, seamless, synergy,
empower(ment), unlock, leverage (as a verb), cutting-edge, one-stop shop, "in today's market",
exclamation-point hype.

---

## CTAs

Direct, first-person or imperative, outcome-focused. Keep the set small and consistent:
**Get my rate**, **Apply now**, **Talk to us**, **See loan programs**, **Read the guide**.
Avoid "Submit", "Learn more" (use a specific verb), and "Click here".

---

## Before / after

- ❌ "USAM Fund provides best-in-class financing solutions to meet the diverse needs of today's
  real estate investors." 
  ✅ "We fund flips, rentals, new builds, and bridge deals — fast, with our own capital."

- ❌ "Submit your information and a representative will reach out at their earliest convenience."
  ✅ "Tell us about the deal. A real person gets back to you, usually same day."

- ❌ "Our innovative DSCR solution empowers investors to unlock their portfolio potential."
  ✅ "A DSCR loan qualifies on the property's rent, not your tax returns — so you can keep scaling."

---

## Quick checklist (use on every piece)

1. Could a sharp investor have said this to a peer? (If it sounds like a bank, rewrite.)
2. Is it in active voice, leading with the point?
3. Did I cut the filler and the hype words?
4. Are trade-offs and guardrails honest and visible?
5. Numerals for numbers, no en dashes, contractions on, exclamation points off?
6. Is the CTA specific and from the approved set?
